Risk Matrix

MoSCoW-prioritised risks with mitigation strategies

HIGH
GDPR Article 22 — Automated Decision-Making
Using ML predictions to trigger customer interventions may constitute automated decision-making under GDPR. Requires explicit legal basis and human oversight mechanism.
Mitigation: Legal review pre-deployment · Human-in-the-loop for all high-risk scores · Documented explainability framework · DPO sign-off required (Must Have)
HIGH
Model Bias — Gender & Geography
Female gender and German geography are significant predictors. Risk that model reinforces discriminatory treatment if interventions differ by these characteristics.
Mitigation: Fairness audit before deployment · Monitor intervention rates by demographic segment · Equality Act 2010 compliance review (Must Have)
MEDIUM
Model Drift — Changing Customer Behaviour
Model trained on historical data may degrade in accuracy as market conditions, product mix, or customer demographics shift over time.
Mitigation: Quarterly model retraining schedule · Performance monitoring dashboard · Accuracy threshold alerts (Should Have)
MEDIUM
Low Front-line Adoption
CRM team may not trust or act on model predictions, reducing ROI. Change management and training are critical success factors.
Mitigation: Pilot with 2 CRM team leads · Simple risk score UI (not raw probabilities) · Training programme · Champion/challenger approach (Should Have)
LOW
Data Quality Degradation
Missing or stale customer data could reduce prediction accuracy over time, particularly for balance and activity fields.
Mitigation: Data quality checks in pipeline · Alert on missing value rates above 5% threshold (Could Have)

BA Recommendations

MoSCoW-prioritised actions for leadership

Must Have
Activate model on inactive customers immediately
Inactive members churn at 2.6x the rate of active ones. A targeted re-engagement campaign using model scores on the inactive segment can recover an estimated 280–340 customers in Q1 alone.
Est. impact: ↓ 8–12% overall churn rate · £580K revenue protected
Must Have
Address Germany market — pricing or product review
German customers churn at 32.4% vs 16.2% in France and 16.8% in Spain. Geography is the 5th most important predictor. A market-specific retention strategy is required before model deployment in Germany.
Est. impact: ↓ German churn from 32% → 20% · £310K revenue protected
Should Have
Simplify 3–4 product customers' experience
Customers holding 3 or more products show dramatically elevated churn. This counterintuitive finding suggests product complexity, not loyalty, is driving churn at the upper end. UX and onboarding review needed.
Est. impact: ↓ 4–6% churn in multi-product segment · £210K revenue protected
Could Have
Age-based segmentation for retention offers
Customers aged 45–60 are the highest-risk age band at 38.4% churn. Tailored retention offers (premium service tier, dedicated relationship manager) for this segment could meaningfully reduce age-driven churn.
Est. impact: ↓ 3–5% churn in 45–60 segment · £175K revenue protected
